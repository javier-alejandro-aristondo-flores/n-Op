# Standing traps register

Durable findings that must never be re-violated. Each was found by an adversarial
audit, and most were found *after* something had already been stated wrongly in
the corpus. This register exists because these recur: they are the failure modes
that survive ordinary review, because the wrong version looks exactly as
plausible as the right one.

**Read this before changing anything involving a sign, a unit, a reference frame,
or a validity boundary.**

Status: **enforced** = the rule is stated at the cited canonical page and a
consumer reading canon cannot get it wrong. **advisory** = correct here, but not
yet mechanically enforced anywhere.

---

## A. Sign conventions

1. **AlGaN P_sp bowing sign.** The reference CSV uses `+b·x(1−x)` with **b
   positive**; FB01 Eq. 4–5 defines the same physics as `−b·x(1−x)` with **b
   negative** ("bowing always upward"). Equivalent via the double negative.
   *Breaks:* lifting one convention's `b` into the other's form inverts the
   bowing and corrupts interface charge at mid-to-high Al. — enforced,
   [accuracy-ledger]

2. **Pyroelectric coefficient sign.** Seeded III-N `P_sp` are negative in the
   ZB-reference frame and `|P_sp|` decreases with rising T, so **`p = dP_sp/dT` is
   positive** in the seeded frame. Raw literature quotes `p` negative under the
   positive-`P_s` convention. *Breaks:* n_s(T) drift — a 15–30% effect over
   harsh-environment ΔT — runs the wrong direction. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

3. **Breakdown-field temperature slope.** `κ_BR > 0`: E_b **rises** with T
   (diamond +5×10⁻⁴/K, 4H-SiC +7×10⁻⁴/K). The earlier "drops ~20%" conflated E_b
   with mobility collapse. *Breaks:* every high-temperature device claim predicts
   softening where the material hardens. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

4. **Misfit strain sign.** Normalized corpus-wide to
   `ε_misfit = (a_film − a_sub)/a_sub`. *Breaks:* Matthews–Blakeslee critical
   thickness and every heterostructure strain sign. — advisory

5. **e₁₅ piezoelectric sign is literature-split (±)** and is recorded as a GAP,
   never seeded. *Breaks:* silently picking a sign gives a wrong shear-piezo
   response with no provenance trail. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

6. **Entropy-production direction.** The H-theorem gives `dS/dt ≥ 0`, so `−S[f]`
   is non-**increasing**. *Breaks:* the monotonicity an entropy-monotone
   integrator or residual would enforce. — advisory

## B. Units, dimensions, uncertainty encodings

7. **Frenkel-pair yield needs the macroscopic displacement cross-section**
   `Σ_d = N_atom·σ_d` (cm⁻¹). Without it the expression is a fluence (cm⁻²), not
   a concentration. *Breaks:* the radiation channel is dimensionally invalid. —
   enforced, [arch-21-multiscale-state]

8. **Three σ encodings, and `—` means unassigned.** Absolute σ / multiplicative
   `×N` (σ_ln = ln N) / `unbounded`. A bare `—` row **cannot** back a
   `ProvenanceLedger` coefficient. *Breaks:* consumers dispatch on the wrong σ
   format, or a cert run refuses the compositions a seeding wave existed to
   enable. — enforced, [reference-battery]

9. **Thermal-expansion tensor form** uses **compliance** `S = C⁻¹` and the `1/V`
   prefactor. *Breaks:* dimensionally wrong α, propagating into gap-vs-T strain,
   G(T), and the T,P hull. — advisory

10. **e-ph vertex normalization.** The `√(2Mω)` single-mass shorthand is valid for
    one species only; multi-species cells need mass-weighted eigenvectors.
    *Breaks:* per-species mass error in every e-ph matrix element. — advisory

11. **Gaussian units and the 4π in the Maxwell source term.** *Breaks:* factor-4π
    errors across the EM sector. — advisory

## C. Reference frames and pairing rules

12. **Polarization-convention pairing — the flagship rule.** Use *exactly one* of
    (a) ZB-reference `P_sp` + **proper** e₃₁ + no ZB-correction, or (b)
    layered-hexagonal `P_sp` + ΔP_corr + **improper** e₃₁. Never mix: improper
    e₃₁ is ≈**3.4×** proper for GaN/AlN. *Breaks:* 2DEG sheet density silently
    corrupted ~3× in the piezo term. — enforced, [arch-12-cert] §12.0.3

13. **The ±5% ΔP target rests on an accidental cancellation, not a generic one.**
    The spurious ZB-reference term and the proper/improper error are large,
    opposite-sign, and nearly cancel **for AlGaN/GaN only**. Experimentally
    reinforced: off-axis holography shows ZB-frame theory bows with the *opposite
    curvature sign* to measurement for InGaN — **bowing is not
    reference-invariant**. *Breaks:* extending ±5% to other alloy systems, or
    importing a bowing curvature across reference frames. — enforced,
    [accuracy-ledger]

14. **AHC ZPR must be the isochoric value, and tagged.** The `coth` dressing is
    pure electron-phonon; lattice expansion belongs to the thermal-expansion row.
    A `total` tag co-activated with it is **refused**. The tag rides the ZPR
    *amplitude*, not a total shift at temperature. *Breaks:* dE_g/dT
    double-counted; n_i is exponentially sensitive to E_g. — enforced,
    [arch-12-cert] §12.0.3

15. **Diamond's direct-gap ZPR stays quarantined from the indirect** — different
    valley. *Breaks:* ~2× overstated renormalization on the load-bearing indirect
    gap. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

16. **β-Ga₂O₃: three axis systems, never interchanged.** The crystal-physical
    frame is `e₂∥b`, `e₃∥c`, `e₁ = a*`, so `λ₂₂ ≡ [010]` and `λ₃₃ ≡ [001]` — but
    **plane-normal** measurements `λ₍₁₀₀₎`/`λ₍₀₀₁₎` lie along **a\***/**c\*** and
    must never be relabelled `[100]`/`[001]`. The high-field third axis is **c\***
    (≈13.8° off `[001]`). **A fourth frame is live in the reference data**: the
    elastic tensors are seeded in `x∥a y∥b z∥c*` (RUS+LDI), whose first axis is the
    *real* `a`, not `a*` — the two differ by 13.83°, so `C₁₁` there is not along the
    crystal-physical `e₁`. Four frames, one material; every value carries its frame
    or it is not usable. *Breaks:* in a ~2.5×-anisotropic material, values attach
    to the wrong crystallographic direction. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

17. **Never average polarized quantities.** β-Ga₂O₃'s absorption onset is
    polarization-dependent (~0.3 eV spread). Seed the effective gap *and* the
    onsets as separate rows. *Breaks:* a single averaged gap matching no
    measurement, defeating direction-resolved certification. — advisory

18. **Diamond electron affinity is termination-dependent** — every use must be
    termination-tagged. *Breaks:* Schottky-barrier and leakage paths silently pick
    a surface that is not the device's. — advisory

## D. Cert-refusal boundaries

19. **Unprovenanced coefficient ⇒ refuse the composition.** Any active channel
    without a `ProvenanceLedger` entry `(value, σ, source, cost-class)` refuses.
    *Breaks:* a silent accuracy hole ships as a confident number. — enforced,
    [arch-12-cert] §12.0.3

20. **No breakdown claim without that material's provenanced `(a,b)` for that
    carrier.** A contested-but-provenanced value does not refuse; it flags.
    *Breaks:* breakdown predicted for materials with no measured α_ii. —
    enforced, [arch-12-cert] §12.0.3

21. **>500 °C breakdown is cert-refused / frontier — not a "±20% met" target.**
    The EDF-tail anchor data do not exist. *Breaks:* the project's headline
    harsh-environment claim asserted where nothing anchors it. — enforced,
    [accuracy-ledger]

22. **β-Ga₂O₃ holes: cert-refuse, do not seed.** Flat valence bands, small-polaron
    self-trapping, no band-like p-transport; the ~3.5 eV UV luminescence is
    free-electron ↔ STH recombination, **not** band-edge. *Breaks:* seeding a hole
    mobility / α_ii / C–T set for a material that has none. — enforced,
    [arch-17-out-of-scope]

23. **AlN measured avalanche α_ii is a genuine GAP** (Monte-Carlo, electron-only);
    *measured* stays cert-refused. *Breaks:* an MC number presented as
    measurement-grade. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

## E. Verifier-soundness requirements

24. **γ̂ admissibility must be scored, not presupposed:** `γ̂† = γ̂`,
    `0 ⪯ γ̂ ⪯ 1`, `Tr γ̂ = N_e`, idempotency only when T=0 is claimed. *Breaks:* a
    candidate zeroes every EOM residual while being an unphysical density matrix —
    the oracle stops being sound as a verifier. — enforced, [arch-11-residuals]

25. **Gauge and electrostatic partition.** Weyl gauge `A₀ ≡ 0`; transverse sector
    in `E_EM`; longitudinal/electrostatic owned by Hartree + ion–ion. *Breaks:*
    electrostatic energy double-counted and `EOM/A` gauge-dependent. — enforced,
    [arch-04-state], [arch-05-generic]

26. **Two independent "polar" predicates.** `is-polar-material` (Born charges /
    LO–TO) gates Fröhlich/POP/LST; `is-noncentrosymmetric` (point group) gates
    P_sp/piezo/pyro/2DEG. They coincide on diamond and wurtzite III-N and **split
    on β-Ga₂O₃** — centrosymmetric, yet Fröhlich-dominated. *Breaks:* one gate
    either invents spontaneous polarization for Ga₂O₃ or kills its dominant
    scattering channel. — enforced, [arch-13-applicability]

27. **`ω² ≥ 0` must be applicability-gated to claimed-stable phases.** *Breaks:*
    legitimate saddle points — which NEB paths must traverse — score as
    violations. — enforced, [arch-11-residuals]

28. **The hull residual must be T,P-aware with a metastability band**, so
    metastable diamond reads R = 0. *Breaks:* a naive hull tells the operator the
    MVP material should not exist. — enforced, [arch-11-residuals]

29. **Callaway-vs-BTE is a consistency pair, not an equivalence pair.** No
    agreement theorem exists for model-vs-microscopic pairs; it trips only on
    *excess*, and is circular if τ is fitted to the BTE. *Breaks:* an obligation
    that either always trips or is vacuously satisfied. — enforced,
    [arch-12-cert] §12.0.2

30. **Learned corrections must be frozen w.r.t. the loss they modify.** The
    EDF-tail `Δα` is fit only to external anchors; with no anchors it **ships as
    identity** and the corner stays cert-refused. *Breaks:* the model co-adapts
    the correction to zero its own residual, destroying supervision exactly in the
    unmeasured corner the validity domain protects. — enforced, [arch-12-cert]
    §12.0.3

31. **Drift-diffusion face flux must be Scharfetter–Gummel, not central
    differencing.** At MV/cm with ~10 nm cells the cell Péclet number is ≈40.
    *Breaks:* the **residual operator itself** is wrong at the operating point, so
    the operator is scored against a discretization artifact. — enforced,
    [arch-21-multiscale-state]

32. **PSD is a condition on the assembled dissipative super-block per mechanism,
    and the projector must be the congruence-action Reynolds operator** — a bare
    orthogonal projection does not preserve PSD. *Breaks:* per-kernel PSD holds
    while global `M ⪰ 0` fails; entropy production goes negative. — enforced,
    [arch-19-coupling-structure] (assembly) and [arch-12-cert] (the `PSDSymmForm`
    obligation that checks it)

33. **Swept-environment validity windows are re-evaluated per training sample**,
    each kernel tagged with the `Environment` box its structure is valid on.
    *Breaks:* a T-sweep crosses a QHA / Chynoweth / `ω²≥0` boundary and leaves a
    stale kernel silently in force. — enforced, [arch-13-applicability]

## F. Known-wrong-if-lifted-naively

34. **The κ errors do not cancel — decouple them.** RTA *under*estimates diamond κ
    by 30–50% near 300 K; missing 4-phonon *over*estimates ~1% at 300 K rising to
    ~30% at 1000 K. Anchor to `κ_iter`, not `κ_RTA`. The dramatic room-temperature
    4-phonon collapse is **BAs, not diamond**. Every κ column must declare its
    isotope / boundary / RTA-vs-iterative scope. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

35. **3-phonon BTE overpredicts GaN/AlN at high T** (measured GaN κ ∝ T^−1.2→−1.5).
    **AlN high-T is theory-only** — no single-crystal measurement above ~500 K.
    *Breaks:* III-N thermal budgets optimistic by ~1.7× at the operating point. —
    enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

36. **Impact-ionization prefactors span >4 orders of magnitude** ⇒ σ ≥ **×3**,
    never ×1.5. *Breaks:* E_b enters BFOM cubed, so a falsely tight α_ii σ
    produces a falsely tight figure of merit. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

37. **Image-force lowering** is `Δφ = √(qE/4πε_sε₀)` — diamond 0.16 eV at
    10⁶ V/cm. Two wrong values previously circulated (a √10 field-scaling error,
    and one ~13% high). *Breaks:* φ_B-derived contact resistance shifts by
    `e^(Δ/kT)`. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

38. **GaN 2.5×10⁷ cm/s is the PEAK velocity, not saturation**; and **μ_n(AlN)
    ≈300 is doped/defective, not intrinsic**. *Breaks:* velocity and mobility
    ceilings overstated ~2× and ~3×. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

39. **β-Ga₂O₃ critical field is an anisotropic triple, not a scalar** —
    10.2/4.8/7.6 MV/cm along a/b/c\*. The widely quoted "≈8 MV/cm" appears nowhere
    in the paper it is attributed to. *Breaks:* the scalar overstates the weak (b)
    axis — the one that actually limits the device — by **1.67×** (8 vs 4.8), and
    understates `a` by 1.28×. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

40. **QHA validity is per-material and does not follow a Θ_D-scaled rule.**
    Diamond (Θ_D ≈ 2200 K) holds through ~800 °C; GaN (Θ_D ≈ 600 K) fails near
    500 °C; AlN (Θ_D ≈ 1000 K) also fails at 500 °C. Those are 0.49, 1.29 and 0.77
    of Θ_D respectively — no single fraction fits, and the "~Θ_D/2" rule this
    register used to state was back-fitted from diamond alone and contradicted its
    own GaN example (Θ_D/2 for GaN is 27 °C, not 500 °C). **"QHA suffices" is a
    diamond-only claim; for III-N, 500 °C is the boundary and it is measured, not
    derived.** *Breaks:* thermal expansion, gap-vs-T strain, G(T) and the hull for
    the flagship polar materials at the design point. — enforced,
    [arch-17-out-of-scope]

41. **Alloy-disorder scattering is the dominant AlGaN mobility limiter.**
    *Breaks:* μ(AlGaN) systematically optimistic for the flagship device's channel
    material. — enforced, [accuracy-ledger]

42. **Degenerate p⁺ diamond breaks two non-degenerate assumptions:** the Einstein
    relation is a declared model-form error, and LST-derived ε_r + Fröhlich
    screening must be gated on `n < n_degenerate`. *Breaks:* standard p⁺ contact
    layers modeled with the wrong diffusion constant and an out-of-validity
    dielectric function. — enforced, [arch-21-multiscale-state],
    [arch-17-out-of-scope]

43. **Diamond's high-T failure mode is air-oxidation (~600–700 °C), not
    graphitization (~1500 °C in vacuum).** *Breaks:* an ~800 °C error in the
    stated operating ceiling. — enforced, [mvp-01-system]

44. **Worked examples hide errors**, and they hide them in more than one file at
    a time. The 2026-07-07 gap audit found, in [deriv-defects] B.3/B.4, an exact
    charge-balance formula with the exponential factor and `g_d` misplaced —
    evaluating **~100× low** (1.9e15 vs 1.9e17) — alongside an inverted activation
    fraction that was ~3× low. Separately, in [deriv-generator-catalog] §6, one
    worked example fired Fröhlich + POP-limited `v_sat` on **non-polar diamond**
    (violating its own `is-polar-material` classifier), fed the ~4.5 eV *n*-type
    barrier into leakage for a *p*-type contact (holes see `φ_B^p = E_g − φ_B^n`
    ≈ 1.0 eV, so ~3.5 eV wrong), used the 0.06 eV image-force value (a √10 field
    error; 0.16 eV at 10⁶ V/cm), and quoted κ_L(773 K) ≈ 800 against a battery
    value of 620. *Breaks:* examples that look pedagogical and teach five wrong
    things, in two files, from one bad afternoon. Do not read "the worked example"
    as a single artifact. — advisory

## G. Practice traps

45. **A prior audit's clearance is not evidence.** A pass cleared κ(T) and
    high-field parameters as "SOUND" and missed a κ overprediction, a
    mis-citation, and a **fabricated** citation. Re-verify *values*, never
    verdicts. — enforced, [audit-prompt] §5

46. **Seed from the ledger and reference CSVs, never from an appendix
    derivation.** Appendix pages rank lowest in the authority order and carry
    superseded values behind changelogs. *Breaks:* a wave silently reverts a
    landed fix. — enforced, [conventions]

47. **Registry formula names are hash-consed into content addresses.** A
    post-landing rename is a substrate-wide rekey, not an edit. Fix names before
    they land. — enforced, [conventions]

48. **Structural index analysis is generic-values-only** — it can pass while the
    Jacobian is singular at the operating point. Pair structural witnesses with
    sampled numerical spot-checks; failures refuse loudly. *Breaks:* an evolver
    certified steppable that is singular exactly where the device operates. —
    advisory

49. **Surrogate rows are refused inside any evolver RHS.** A surrogate's forward
    relaxation bias becomes model error *of the vector field* and compounds along
    trajectories. *Breaks:* a relaxation acceptable for a pointwise score
    integrates into a divergent trajectory. — advisory

50. **AD-safety must be read as trajectory-safety for an evolver.** Rewrites exact
    almost-everywhere but wrong on measure-zero sets are tolerable for a pointwise
    scorer, not for a flow attracted to the bad set. *Breaks:* a rewrite safe for
    scoring corrupts a trajectory. — advisory

51. **Pin provenance before any ledger entry; record unresolved conflicts as
    ranges, not picks.** Open pins include contested Schottky barriers, carbide
    onsets, and a Curie point sitting inside the operating window. *Breaks:* a
    contested value enters the ledger as fact and propagates into contact
    resistance and lifetime figures of merit. — enforced, [reference-battery]

52. **A passing adjoint gate is not evidence of a correct `D2` tag.** vJp and JvP
    agree trivially wherever the true gradient is zero, so a row whose output is
    piecewise constant — an argmin over a discrete set, a hard-cutoff integer
    count — **passes the registration gate spuriously** and ships a certificate
    for a gradient that does not exist. Rows 46 and 50 did exactly that.
    *Breaks:* the one gate that is supposed to catch a missing gradient issues a
    pass instead. — enforced, [impl-07-residual-factory] §7.5

53. **`D3` is a structural claim, not a fallback tier.** It asserts the output is
    a converged fixed point whose adjoint costs one linear solve *independent of
    forward iteration count*. The retired S5 catalog legend used `D3` for
    "finite-difference fallback only", and `D0` for "closed-form analytical
    gradient" — the opposite of what `D0` means now. Migrating a `Diff` cell out
    of that legend without translating it is how rows 12 and 106 were registered
    `D3` while being a finite difference and a transient PDE respectively.
    *Breaks:* a row promises an iteration-count-independent adjoint it cannot
    deliver. — enforced, [impl-04-formulas]

54. **`DN` asserts that no relaxation exists** — not merely that none has been
    written. It is the strongest claim in the differentiability vocabulary and
    therefore the one most often wrong. Row 85 sat at `DN` (then spelled `DX`)
    while the corpus already prescribed its descriptor-distance relaxation, and
    row 101 sat there while row 113 was taking a gradient through a Berry phase.
    Check for a real-valued output component and for an existing relaxation before
    assigning it. *Breaks:* an MVP-critical row silently leaves the differentiable
    path. — enforced, [impl-04-formulas]

55. **A `D4` row whose `source` cell names no relaxation is un-gateable.** The
    obligation-9 approval has nothing to approve and the model-form bias never
    enters `combineTol`. *Breaks:* a relaxation ships with an undeclared,
    unbounded bias. — enforced, `seams.py` check (g)

56. **Diff tags collide with real physics and must be backticked.** `DX` was the
    tag for "no useful derivative" *and* the name of a deep-donor configuration
    this corpus analyses in AlGaN and AlN; the tag was renamed `DN`. `D1`–`D5`
    remain the wurtzite deformation potentials in [accuracy-ledger]. Write tags in
    backticks so a search for one does not return the other. *Breaks:* a sweep
    over "all `D3` rows" silently includes a deformation-potential table. —
    enforced, [conventions]

57. **A rename must leave a forwarding address.** Registry names are content
    addresses, and a rename that lands only in the CSV leaves every prose mention
    pointing at nothing. `physics/library/formulas/retired-names.csv` maps every
    retired name to what it landed as, and `seams.py` fails on any retired name a
    page mentions without resolving it in the same paragraph. *Breaks:* a reader
    greps a name from an appendix and concludes the row does not exist. —
    enforced, [conventions]

58. **A checker that finds nothing may simply not be looking.** `apparatus.py`
    harvested section coordinates with a dot-requiring pattern, so every
    `[timeline] §<date>` citation — about twenty — was silently skipped, and a
    green run was cited as evidence they resolved. `seams.py` captured the
    argument of a `formula =` slot with an ASCII-only character class, so an
    argument written `σ/(n·e)` was invisible to it. Both were green the whole time. **Before citing a clean run as
    evidence, plant a defect of exactly the class you are claiming is absent and
    confirm the checker fails.** *Breaks:* "clearance is not evidence" applied to
    the tools that produce the clearance. — enforced, [audit-prompt] §5


59. **`GAP` means three different things in this corpus.** (i) The
    **missing-data marker** in the reference-data CSVs and the accuracy ledger —
    a value that is genuinely unavailable and is cert-refused rather than
    guessed; this is the load-bearing sense. (ii) **GAP the computer-algebra
    system**, a candidate for the offline group-theory role
    ([arch-18-open-decisions]). (iii) **GAP the Gaussian Approximation
    Potential**, an ML interatomic potential, in the CSP methodology survey
    ([deriv-csp] Part A). And unbacked lowercase `gap` is almost always the band
    gap. *Breaks:* a sweep for missing data collects a language candidate and an
    ML potential; a sweep for the band gap collects all four. Write the marker in
    caps and the software with its full name. — advisory
