# Library landscape

`n-Op` is partitioned into three sibling libraries.

- **`/physics`** — the reference oracle, **numerics-agnostic at its seam**
  (pure function, flat arrays, no loop ownership) while internally committed to
  the representation substrate of `[representation-substrate]`. It encodes the
  laws of the system: state structure, dynamics, observable definitions,
  residual definitions, and certification obligations. It does **not** hold
  time-varying state values, train neural networks, integrate trajectories, or
  wrap external DFT codes at runtime. This book is primarily about `/physics`.
- **`/informed-operator`** — the PINO itself. It consumes `/physics` and learns
  the time-evolution operator. Its seam contract and loss methodology live under
  `informed-operator/design/`.
- **`/interface`** — owns every **driving loop**: training, design search,
  active learning. Not yet designed. Note the oracle's own CLI — `compile`,
  `inspect`, `validate` (`[product]`) — ships *inside* `/physics`; `/interface`
  is the loops, not the command line.

Engineering aspects (defects, dopants, surfaces, interfaces, operating-condition
effects) live **inside** `/physics`, not in a separate library.
