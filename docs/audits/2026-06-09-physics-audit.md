# Deep audit of `/physics` — physics completeness, correctness, and computational soundness

**Date:** 2026-06-09 · **Auditor:** senior computational-physics / scientific-computing review (commissioned per `AUDIT_PROMPT.md`) · **Corpus audited:** all 37 atomic spec files (architecture 2,660 ln; implementation 905; mvp 237), `computational-overview.md`, `formula-registry.md`, `properties.md`, `registry-manifest.csv` (104 rows), all 10 `physics/research/*` files (4,485 ln), meta tooling, git history. Coverage: 100% of files read in full; every defect-class finding re-verified against cited text; the four highest-stakes findings adversarially attacked by independent skeptic reviews (results in §8).

**Verdict classes:** `defect` (wrong/missing, must fix) · `risk` (judgment call worth surfacing) · `open` (intentionally deferred — not counted against the spec) · `verified-ok` (affirmatively checked sound).
**Severity:** **S1** invalidates a design commitment at its stated scope · **S2** materially wrong/missing physics for a target material/regime · **S3** ambiguity/inconsistency that would mislead an implementer · **S4** editorial.
**Scope tags:** MVP (diamond slice) / V1 (full committed spec) / V2 / vision (stated ambitions beyond V1).
**Design commitments audited against:** **C1** = design-grade accuracy without expensive runtime numerics (closed-form/one-shot scoring + compile-time amortization); **C2** = one maximal canonical structure, specialized down.

## 1. Executive verdict

`/physics` is an unusually disciplined specification whose **committed core is physically sound and whose central computational bet is structurally correct** — but it is **not yet the general-purpose UWBG-durability residual calculator it claims to be**, and the distance is measurable. The architecture's deepest insight survives adversarial scrutiny: because the PINO supplies the full candidate state (including `γ̂`), `/physics` *scores* law-violation instead of *solving* laws, so the runtime hot path genuinely avoids iterative numerics (the registry's own row 71, `(j,E,T_L,σ,κ) → ‖PDE‖`, is the pattern in miniature); expensive work (DFT/DFPT reference solves, `E_BO` evaluations, NEB, G₀W₀) is real but per-composition, which is exactly the amortization C1 promised — although the spec's own cost table ("seconds–minutes" compile, "µs–ms" runtime, arch-07 §7.6) misstates both ends, and the evaluation-cadence policy that reconciles them was researched (catalog §3.4) but lost in synthesis. The MVP physics (structure/phonons/G₀W₀ bands/equilibrium defects/3-ph RTA transport/compact-model device layer) is correct at **TCAD-grade, not ab-initio-grade**, accuracy. Three structural gaps gate the stated objective: **(i) the degradation/aging pipeline — the durability physics itself — is researched in depth but essentially absent from the committed surface** (no defect generation–annihilation kinetics, no oxidation or H-desorption rates despite the project's own catalog naming them *the* lifetime limiters, no radiation anywhere — a silent scope drop absent from both the registry and the arch-17 exclusion register); **(ii) polarization/piezoelectric/2DEG physics is missing** (a fifth, *untracked* formalization gap that makes the flagship GaN/AlN/AlGaN device class non-computable as specified); **(iii) the unit-cell ↔ device-scale bridge is undefined** (the research flagged "bridging to device-scale is its own decision"; the decision was never made — arch-18 §2's "PDE-mesh format" understates it). A cluster of formal defects in the GENERIC layer (degeneracy/level-scoping as written, PSD-assembly granularity, Jacobi unaddressed, differentiability-tag legend drift) are spec-level rather than physics-level and are fixable by normalization, not re-research.

**Per-commitment verdict.** C1: **holds at MVP scope** (score-not-solve + small IFT-adjointed fixed points + per-composition references), *conditional* on restoring the cadence policy and re-scoping the cost table; the un-costed item threatening C1 at V1-generality is per-material coupling-coefficient provenance (DFPT/GW runs), which lives outside the costed pipeline. C2: **holds** — the substrate genuinely covers every load-bearing object I could find, with exactly the two declared sui-generis exceptions (the future PDE mesh is the tracked third); the one real leak in "specialize-down" is that coupling *values* (vs forms) enter via `ProvenanceLedger`, outside the canonical generative structure.

**Confidence.** High (mechanical/joins-based) for all completeness, count, and synthesis-loss findings — these were established by deterministic cross-referencing, not recall. Medium-high (independent domain knowledge) for quantitative accuracy claims (κ-RTA/4-ph magnitudes, gap-ZPR, mesh convergence) — argued from literature-standard values, not recomputed here. Medium for the GENERIC-formalism judgments, where two skeptic reviews materially weakened my initial severities (recorded in §8) and residual interpretive ambiguity is itself the finding. **Is it ready to build?** The substrate, pipeline, and MVP slice: yes, with the §7 P0-list fixed first (none requires research). The V1 "comprehensive spec" claim: no — the three structural gaps above must be either specified or explicitly de-scoped before "comprehensiveness is the point" (arch-01) is true.

## 2. Part 1 — Physics findings (ranked by severity)

### S1/S2 — structural completeness

**P1.1 · The degradation pipeline is missing — the objective's core physics. — defect, S1(vision)/S2(V1), conf H.**
The stated purpose is *durable* devices (arch-01:18-21). The research corpus treats degradation seriously: defect populations must be "a state variable … evolving by generation–annihilation kinetics, not an equilibrium quantity" with sources `G_thermal + G_carbide_front + G_ion_flux` (`defects-surfaces-interfaces.md:54, Part G`); H-redistribution is "the silent killer" (G.2); platelet clustering (G.3), vibration-driven dislocation multiplication (G.7); the observable catalog names air-oxidation ">600 °C *the* lifetime limiter" (#46) and H-desorption the χ-shift driver (#47), plus defect-evolution kinetics (#51). What landed: an *equilibrium* charge-balance closure (`SelfConsistentChargeBalanceOf`) and three B11 rows (carbide 81, electromigration 82, fatigue 83). No generation–annihilation channel, no oxidation/H-desorption rates, no frozen-in-vs-equilibrium population distinction (`R_ThermalCycleStability`, csp E.1), no radiation (next finding). The B9 formalization gap (§13) covers some of this *nominally*, but the missing content here exceeds "enumeration": there is no state mechanism for slowly-evolving defect populations at all (`SiteDecoration.occupancy` is static input). **Recommendation:** add a `DefectPopulation` slow-state schema (or sanction it as an L4 distribution-state instance), register the Part-G kinetics formulas (Arrhenius generation, H-diffusion, platelet nucleation, oxidation/H-desorption Eyring rates — all researched with barriers), and make B11 the bundle the project's objective says it must be.

**P1.2 · Radiation: a silent scope drop. — defect, S2, conf H (mechanical).**
arch-01 names radiation as a target condition; `Environment` (arch-03:25-27) has no radiation field/dose; the registry has zero radiation rows; arch-17 does not list radiation among exclusions; §13 does not list it among gaps. Yet NRT displacement and Frenkel-pair-yield formulas are fully researched (`non-equilibrium-high-field.md` Part H; `uwbg-observable-catalog.md` #42 E_d with values). This fails the spec's own openness discipline: it is neither in scope nor out of scope. **Recommendation:** one-line decision — either add the two researched formulas + an `Environment.radiation_flux` field (cheap; they are T0 closed-form), or add radiation to arch-17 explicitly.

**P1.3 · Polar/piezo/2DEG physics absent — fifth, untracked formalization gap. — defect, S2 (S1 at vision scope), conf H.**
No spontaneous-polarization (Berry-phase) or piezoelectric-tensor formula; no 2DEG sheet-density composition (catalog #35, #52, FoM table — the defining observables of AlGaN/GaN HEMTs); no piezoelectric (acoustic long-range) e-ph channel in the arch-19 §19.10 mechanism table; no interface-trap-density `D_it` row (kills the SS FoM). Skeptic review (§8.3) confirms: rows 92–94 (Z*, ε∞, χ∞) provide ingredients from which P_sp could be composed via a λ-path integration (Bernardini–Fiorentini–Vanderbilt style), but no composition spells it out, and the catalog's own "flag for confirmation" (#35) was never resolved. Unlike the four tracked gaps, this one appears in no open list. **Recommendation:** add `spontaneous-polarization` (Berry-phase/λ-path), `piezoelectric-tensor`, `2DEG-sheet-density`, `D_it` rows + one `piezoelectric e-ph, LongRangeStatic(1)` channel row; track in §13 until done.

**P1.4 · The micro↔device scale bridge is undecided. — defect, S2 (S1 if device-level FoMs are V1 deliverables), conf H.**
B9/Cap-3 residuals presuppose continuum fields — `T_L(r)` in row 71's signature, `E(x)` in the avalanche integral (row 75), device-mesh PDE residuals — that the 7-tuple unit-cell state can neither represent nor evolve. The research flags it precisely: "Continuum-PDE template … device-scale — currently the registry is atomic-scale; **bridging to device-scale is its own decision**" (catalog, registry-gaps list); `non-eq` J.5's "T_L is already in state — encoded in S_vib" does not define an extraction T_L(r,t). arch-18 §2 tracks only the mesh *format*. The L4 state-schema question is the same gap from the other side: group-C's synthesis recommends "three distinct state schemas … and a common residual contract" (group-C:611-614); the spec's `Distribution`/`Coverage`/`CompositionVector` types appear in method signatures with no home in the state architecture, and the 17 categories have no EOM-of-distribution slot (arch-08 says L4 "introduces irreducible state"; arch-11 scores only the 7 components). **Recommendation:** promote to a first-class arch-18 decision: define the L4/continuum state schemas (distribution fields, coverage vectors, temperature/potential fields) as typed substrate fibers with their own EOM residual categories, and specify the homogenization step (unit-cell κ(T), σ(T), α_ii(E) → device PDE coefficients) — that step is where the PINO's microscopic learning becomes device prediction, and right now it exists only by implication.

### S2 — accuracy at the design point

**P1.5 · κ(T) for diamond: RTA + 3-phonon only, validated at the wrong point. — defect, S2, conf H (independent).**
The committed path is DFPT + 3-ph BTE-RTA (impl-06:72-74; mvp-03 Cap 3). For diamond, RTA/SMA *underestimates* κ by ~30–50% near 300 K (normal-process dominance — full iterative LBTE is the standard fix), while missing 4-phonon scattering *overestimates* κ by ~10–15% at 300 K growing to ~25–35% near 1000 K. These partially cancel, uncontrolled. The catalog knows ("±20% τ acceptable for κ within 10%… 4-phonon needed above 1000 K", #12-#13; Part E: 10–20%), but the registry has no 4-ph row, no iterative-LBTE option, and the three documents disagree on the 4-ph disposition (arch-17 "replaced by D4 surrogates"; catalog "learned correction factor >1500 K"; registry: nothing). The MVP battery anchors κ(300 K) — RTA's *worst* point — with no high-T anchor (mvp-05 H4). **Recommendation:** (a) pick one 4-ph disposition and implement it (a Slack-like high-T correction factor with a cert obligation-9-style validity domain is fine and cheap); (b) add iterative-LBTE as the faithful sibling (the collision matrix is already assembled for RTA; the iteration is per-composition); (c) add κ(773 K) and κ(1100 K) battery rows.

**P1.6 · Temperature-dependent band gap (e-ph renormalization) absent. — defect, S2, conf H.**
No AHC/ZPR/Σ_ep row anywhere (CSV scan; impl-06 electronic block). Diamond's zero-point renormalization is famously −0.3 to −0.4 eV; dgap/dT ≈ −0.3…−0.6 meV/K (catalog #1) gives another ~−0.2 eV at 800 K; `n_i(T) ∝ exp(−E_g/2kT)` makes leakage and thermionic rows exponentially sensitive to it. Researched three times over (group-A §3.6 with the AHC arXiv source; catalog #1, #15, Part E mitigation "scissor + AHC"); row 63 captures only the strain part (~30–40% of the shift). **Recommendation:** add an AHC one-shot dressing to Layer 1.25 (it is exactly the "one-shot, closed-form correction" that tier was built for: Σ_qν |g|²-weighted sums over the already-computed phonon and band structures), tag the gap-bearing formulas' applicability with it.

**P1.7 · Transport-mesh convergence and interpolation unaddressed. — defect, S2, conf H.**
μ/κ_e/α_ii from BTE need ~50³-equivalent k-sampling; the MVP's 8×8×8/29-IBZ mesh (mvp-02) is factor-2-grade for transport even with exact inputs, and no Wannier/Fourier interpolation machinery exists in methods/templates (the `Wannier` basis exists only as a γ̂ encoding). Stage-2 IBZ reduction helps cost, not convergence. **Recommendation:** either add band/e-ph interpolation (Wannier or Fourier FC-style, both per-composition compile work — fits C1), or state mesh-convergence error explicitly in the per-observable accuracy ledger and battery tolerances.

**P1.8 · High-field tail: Chynoweth/Caughey-Thomas parameters carry the breakdown physics. — risk, S2, conf M-H.**
B9's compact models (rows 16, 74-79) are TCAD-standard and *appropriate* under C1, but their fitted parameters (a, b, v_sat, β; diamond's are sparse and contested experimentally) bound the achievable accuracy: factor-2 in α_ii ⇒ ~10–20% in E_b (catalog #19-#20 concedes this). The faithful sibling (BTE-full high-energy tail) is named but the EDF-tail machinery (full-band treatment) is explicitly flagged "OUTSIDE registry" (catalog #49) and never resolved. At MVP (diamond, no junction) this is dormant; at V1 device scope it is the breakdown-prediction accuracy ceiling. **Recommendation:** declare parameter provenance + uncertainty per material in the reference cache (the `sigma` column exists — use it for model parameters, not just observables), and keep PINO-learned corrections anchored by the obligation-9 domain mechanism.

**P1.9 · Layer-1.25 dressings are frozen at reference state. — risk, S2/S3, conf M.**
G₀W₀, SCP, Z*, ε∞, LO/TO corrections are one-shot per composition (arch-08 §8.1; OneShotCert: `inputs-hash`, T1|T2). They therefore do not respond to the PINO's state excursions (no gradient contribution; e.g., gap-vs-strain enters only via row 63's separate Ξ). This is a reasonable design trade — but it is nowhere stated as a policy, and P5's error model should carry a "dressing staleness" term (the state-dependence of the dressing is the dropped error). For MVP-scale cells G₀W₀-in-T2 (≤10 s) is plausible; for V1 defect supercells it is hours — the tier table will be violated silently. **Recommendation:** state the frozen-at-reference policy + validity radius explicitly; scope-tag G₀W₀ cost by cell size.

**P1.10 · Residual-category physics quality: strong, with four sharp edges. — mixed, S3.**
The 17 categories (arch-11) cover an impressive constraint set: f-sum, ASR, Kramers–Kronig, Onsager, detailed balance, Einstein, Maxwell relations, method-equivalence — most of what a learning system needs (Q4). Sharp edges: (a) **ω² ≥ 0 positivity** must be applicability-gated to claimed-stable phases or it penalizes legitimate saddle/transition states the trajectories must traverse (arch-11:66; NEB exists in the same spec); (b) **hull-distance** must be the researched T,P-aware, metastability-tolerant form (csp B.2: "diamond is metastable… the residual must use a temperature-and-pressure-aware hull") — a naive hull penalty tells the PINO the MVP material shouldn't exist; (c) **MethodEquivalence** is misapplied to Callaway-vs-BTE (mvp-03 Cap 3): a model-vs-microscopic pair with no agreement theorem (unlike BTE↔Kubo, which itself holds only at matched vertex level, ~10–20%); tolerances are unvalued; circular if τ is fitted to the BTE; (d) **Degeneracy** as a *training* residual is conceptually shaky (see P2.1) — keep it cert-side until the structural fix lands. Also missing as residuals: Wegscheider cycle conditions (researched, group-C 3.4), rotational sum rules (group-A 3.4), T_e ≥ T_L and breakdown-integral guards (researched, non-eq J.4 — currently absent from arch-11's positivity list).

**P1.11 · Material generality beyond diamond. — risk, S2 (vision), conf H.**
GaN/AlN/AlGaN: blocked on P1.3 (+ QHA fails for GaN at exactly 500 °C — Θ_D ≈ 600 K, catalog Part E — so V1's "QHA suffices" is diamond-only; SCPH is V2). β-Ga₂O₃: O missing from the MVP species set (arch-09 §9.7); hole self-trapping (small polarons) excluded by arch-17 ("polaron localization beyond Fröhlich") — defensible and documented, but it forecloses p-side/bipolar breakdown physics for a named target material; anisotropic monoclinic transport is representable (tensor machinery fine). arch-17 also dropped item 12 of the research's own limit list (plasmon–phonon/LST breakdown at >10²⁰ cm⁻³ B-doping — standard p⁺ diamond contacts). Alloy machinery (Vegard 52, Redlich–Kister 95, cluster expansion 84, SQS noted in research) is present and adequate at TCAD grade.

**Verified-ok (physics) worth stating plainly:** diamond MVP anchors numerically correct (gap −23% PBE, Θ_D, a, C_ij; one error: mvp-01:26 conflates air-oxidation ~700 °C with vacuum graphitization ~1500 °C — fix the sentence); Z*=0 applicability masking of rows 17/21/22 is exemplary discipline; FNV/Kumagai/Makov-Payne defect-correction set is state of the art; the SR/LR (deformation-potential + Fröhlich) e-ph split is the correct modern treatment; incomplete-ionization statistics (the "high T activates marginal dopants" inversion — the project's best physics insight) is properly load-bearing in rows 5/35/36; harmonic-TST/Vineyard normalization is correctly product-of-modes; charge-corrections, Schottky (S-M/Bardeen/Cowley-Sze ladder), TBR (AMM/DMM), and the defect inventory are faithful to a high-quality research base.

## 3. Part 2 — Structure and program findings

**P2.1 · The GENERIC layer claims more structure than the written functionals deliver. — defect, S2 (spec-level), conf M-H.**
As written, `L·δS/δx = 0` fails: symplectic `L` on (R,P) against `S_vib(R,h)` gives `(0, −∂S_vib/∂R) ≠ 0`, and the γ̂-block written as `L = (1/iℏ)[Ĥ_KS,·]` (arch-05:69) violates degeneracy off-equilibrium, whereas the Lie–Poisson form `[·, γ̂]` satisfies it (and Jacobi) while reproducing the same flow. Skeptic review (§8.1) correctly notes the rescue: arch-05:83's "each level contributes the … terms that act on its irreducible state" supports a level-scoped reading in which L2 dynamics is Hamiltonian-only (or an isothermal single-generator contraction) and entropy lives with distribution variables — the standard GENERIC-literature resolution. But the spec never *says* which device resolves it; it asserts the two-generator form + degeneracy conditions globally, makes `Degeneracy` a residual category, and promises "Antisymmetry, PSD, Jacobi, degeneracy verified" as a build artifact (impl-10 Phase 8) — unachievable against the written functionals. Jacobi for generated `AntisymmForm` cross-blocks is likewise never addressed in the spec body (antisymmetry alone conserves energy but does not make `L` Poisson); the research states the requirement explicitly (group-C:667). **Recommendation (no new physics needed):** one normative arch-05 subsection declaring (i) per-level generator structure (which E/S terms are active per level; isothermal contraction at L2/L3 if that is the intent), (ii) the γ̂-block as Lie–Poisson, (iii) Jacobi status per L-block (canonical blocks: exact; generated cross-blocks: state assumption or restrict to semidirect-product forms), (iv) `Degeneracy` as cert-only until (i)–(iii) make it identically zero — at which point it is a generator-bug tripwire, which is the useful version.

**P2.2 · PSD enforcement: right core math, wrong granularity, two unstated premises. — defect S3 + verified-ok-with-caveat, conf H.**
Verified: the Reynolds image of a PSD seed is PSD *under the induced congruence action on form-valued targets*, and the dormant Gatermann–Parrilo SDP fallback is correctly specified (arch-19 §19.12). Caveats from the adversarial pass (§8.4): the spec never states that the projector for `PSDSymmForm` targets is the congruence-action Reynolds operator rather than a bare orthogonal subspace projection (only the former preserves PSD); the guard `λ_min(M_block) ≥ −ε` has no value for ε and an ambiguous block; and — the substantive defect — PSD is asserted per off-diagonal *kernel* (§19.1 "lands as an off-diagonal kernel of M") while global `M ⪰ 0` requires Schur-complement/Gram conditions on the assembled dissipative super-block (diagonal + off-diagonal together). The FGR Gram structure cited in [PSD-e-ph] supports exactly the super-block statement — the fix is to *state the condition on the assembled block per dissipative mechanism* and have cert obligation 5 check that. The arch-12 §12.0.1-vs-list obligation-index inconsistency (self-flagged in arch-19:294) is still unfixed.

**P2.3 · The E-functional as written is misleading (downgraded from fatal after skeptic review). — defect, S3, conf H.**
arch-05:49-56 sums `E_BO` and `E_KS[γ̂]` and gives `E_KS` no `(R,h)` argument while the research defines it with `∫v_ext·n + V_II` (group-A:46-48, group-B:40). Under the level-conditional reading (§8.2) there is no double-counting in practice — at L1, `E_KS[γ̂; R₀,h₀]` is parametric in frozen geometry; at L2, `E_BO` replaces it with γ̂ resolved — and Phase-9 dimensional/ASR checks would catch a literal mis-assembly. What survives: the text invites exactly the misreading I made (and an implementer would); the e-ph channel's role (linear-order cross-term for L/M blocks and beyond-reference E_coupling, *not* the full electron–ion term) is implicit; and §19.4's "The author never wrote g. The symmetry group did" overstates — symmetry generates the *form*; the *values* come from `ProvenanceLedger` (fits/DFPT). **Recommendation:** the skeptic's one-sentence fix (state the level-conditional activation + parametric R-dependence of E_KS) plus an honest §19.4 caveat, plus P2.4.

**P2.4 · Coefficient provenance is the un-costed, un-canonical seam. — defect, S2, conf H.**
The generality claim ("any crystalline material") rests on per-material coupling coefficients (deformation potentials, anharmonic force constants, Fröhlich parameters, model parameters of B3/B9) whose acquisition pipeline — DFPT/finite-difference runs, fits — is outside the costed Stage-1–4 pipeline and outside the canonical generative structure (the only "specialize-down" leak in C2). For diamond they are curated; for a new crystal nobody says who computes them, at what cost, with what error, recorded where. **Recommendation:** specify a "parameterization service" contract: per-channel coefficient provenance requirements, cost class (per-material, amortized), uncertainty fields (reuse the battery's σ machinery), and a cert obligation that refuses compositions whose active channels have unprovenanced coefficients.

**P2.5 · Cost/cadence contract: researched, half-lost. — defect, S3 (was S2 before the catalog was credited), conf H.**
The catalog's §3.4 binds tiers to cadence (T0 every step; T1 RAD-subsampled; T2 per-epoch cached; T3 on-demand/calibration-only with cheap proxies during training — explicitly resolving NEGF row 80 and `reference-phase-cache` row 87). The spec kept only `sampling-policy ∈ {UniformBatch, RAD, Importance, ValidationOnly}` (impl-07 §7.1) and one sentence in `formula-registry.md`. Meanwhile arch-07 §7.6's "runtime µs–ms" is true only of the EOM-residual core (BZ-resolved observables are 0.1–10 s on-request; `E_BO`-based property observables are minutes, per-composition), and "compile seconds–minutes" excludes the reference solves the observables require. **Recommendation:** restore the cadence table as normative spec text (one small section in impl-07), and re-state §7.6 as a three-row cost contract: per-sample core / on-request spectral / per-composition property+reference.

**P2.6 · Differentiability-tag semantics drifted between catalog and spec. — defect, S2 (for the affected rows), conf H (mechanical).**
Catalog: D3 = finite-difference fallback, D4 = non-differentiable-needs-relaxation (Gumbel-Softmax etc.). Spec: D3 = implicit-function adjoint, D4 = surrogate/FD (impl-04:42-45). The CSV rows kept catalog tags, so under the spec's own legend the D3 rows — Grüneisen 12, Wulff 45, hull 67, CSP-uniqueness 85, Chern/Z₂/Wilson 99-101 — are nonsense (no fixed point to implicitly differentiate; the integer-valued topology rows have no useful derivative at all and belong in an "exception-set everywhere" class). The catalog's good dispositions (soft-min hull via log-sum-exp; Gumbel-Softmax for row 84) were lost. Also: only 1 D4 row exists vs "~4" claimed, and arch-17's "replaced by D4 surrogates" names surrogates that have no rows. **Recommendation:** re-legend D3/D4 once, re-tag the eight rows, restore the relaxation dispositions, and reconcile arch-17's surrogate claims with actual registry content.

**P2.7 · Substrate, IR, typeclasses: clean, general, verified. — verified-ok, conf H.**
The five-primitive substrate + four op-signatures covers every load-bearing object encountered (vocabularies→Universe, generators→C2, sidecars→C3/HAMT, evidence→C4, addresses→C5, subsets/masks→C6/C7); the two sui-generis exceptions are the right ones, and the only future third (the PDE mesh) is tracked in arch-18 §2. Canonical-serialization rules 1–11 are correct and complete for their stated scope, including the float normalizations; the §20.5 hot-path table is internally consistent and its O(log n) claims are correctly scoped to substrate ops (the looser "always-cheap" kernel discipline is a different, tier-governed claim — the overview/brief blur this; arch-18:101 does not). Hash-consed arena IR with roles-not-kinds for observables/residuals, sidecar stage-poset, and the closed 12-method alphabet absorbing would-be new methods as templates (csp's proposed +3 methods correctly became templates) are exactly the "largest canonical structure, specialize down" discipline working. Minor: arch-20 §20.2 says "three op signatures" then adds a fourth (S4); content-addressing-vs-ε (Q6) is a non-problem at runtime *because nothing float-valued is cache-keyed on the hot path* — caches key on structural fingerprints; the one genuine ε-identity problem (γ̂ e-graph rewriting) is correctly quarantined offline in V1 (arch-15 §15.3/§15.4) — but note Stage-3's main e-graph shares the concern: its rewrites are claimed "exact and AD-safe" (arch-07 §7.3), which holds for hash-consing/CSE but needs a side-condition discipline (positivity/branch-cut guards) once the rule set grows; no rule-soundness contract exists yet (S3, risk).

**P2.8 · Smaller structural defects.** `properties.md` carries an incompatible bundle scheme (B11="topology atlas" vs canon B11=degradation; wrong row ranges) — stale against the arch-09 canon the user's own doc-canon rule names as truth (S3). ## 4. Part 3 — Seeded questions and additional findings

**Q1 (end-to-end error model)** — see §5. Headline: the *algebra* for error composition exists (`Quantity.combineTol`, arch-10) but nothing populates it; most tolerances are unvalued; no composed budget exists.

**Q2 (closed-form boundary).** Classification of the 102 active rows by genuine computational character: ~55 closed-form given inputs (T0); ~25 small-LA/quadrature (T1) — including two honest small *solves* (fermi-level root-find 5, charge-balance fixed point 36, both correctly D2/IFT); ~12 BZ/mesh (T2) including the one-shot dressing computations; 2 T3 (NEGF 80 — coherent-transport Landauer, one linear solve per energy, *not* the excluded self-consistent NEGF; reference-cache 87 — a lookup backed by offline DFT). The iterative methods that remain (method-4 SCF inside `E_BO`, NEB, BTE-full, kMC/MD sampling) are all per-composition/reference-side under the catalog's cadence policy — *no per-sample iterative solve exists in the committed design*, which is C1's real content and it is true.
