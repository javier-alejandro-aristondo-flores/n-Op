# Development timeline

What happened, when, and what survived. Full narrative for every entry is in git
history; this page keeps only what a future reader needs — the outcome, and where
the durable result now lives.

The durable findings themselves are in [traps] — that register, not this
chronology, is the value the audit practice produced.

Headings here are **stable anchors**: cite them as `timeline §<date-slug>`.

## 2026-07-21 — Exact identity, measured error

The γ̂ open questions (`gamma-hat §4`) were four faces of one problem, and two
more of the same species had surfaced this month: no Stage-3 rewrite-soundness
contract, no budget for gradient error under a truncated inner solve.

**The collision.** `representation-substrate §4` makes identity exact — `Address = SHA-256(canonical
bytes)`, floats normalised "so numerically equal values always share an address".
Every object also carries an ε that `combineTol` composes. `≡` is an equivalence
relation; `≈_ε` is a **tolerance** relation and is not transitive, so it induces a
covering by maximal cliques rather than a partition. No partition ⇒ no canonical
representative ⇒ nothing to hash, and hash-consing, Merkle dedup and `O(1)` address
equality all die with it. The substrate needs a quotient; the mathematics offers
only a covering.

**The resolution — one rule.** *Identity is exact, always; ε never enters the
address. Any operation that can make the computed object differ from its exact
counterpart emits a computable a-posteriori estimate of that difference, as cert
evidence.* `≈_ε` is then never used as an identity relation, so its
non-transitivity never has to be repaired. Nothing branches at runtime (the kernel
computes a number); nothing is refused for being approximate (it is *scored*, and
the consumer decides); and the gate gets **weaker** — "must be exact" becomes "must
be able to estimate its own error". No new residual category: these are cert-only
generators, so the 19-tag enum is untouched and the training loss is unpolluted.

**What the literature settled.** Three of the six faces were not open at all.
Robust dynamical-low-rank integrators (projector-splitting, Lubich–Oseledets 2014;
robustness to small singular values, Kieri–Lubich–Walach 2016; rank-adaptive BUG,
Ceruti–Kusch–Lubich 2022) preserve norm, Hamiltonian energy and gradient-flow
monotonicity up to a declared tolerance — trace, the `L` block and the `M` block,
term for term. Inexact implicit differentiation has *computable a-posteriori* error
estimates. Materialization was misfiled: it changes cost, not value, so it carries
no error term at all — it is `revolve`-style checkpointing, a Stage-4 schedule.

And the keystone: e-graph rewriting under float semantics is solved by **side
conditions discharged by an e-class analysis**. Herbie's rules were known-unsound
for years, and deleting them *made the tool useless on a large part of its own
benchmark suite* — exactness-only is not a viable gate. `egglog` (Zhang et al.,
PLDI 2023) made them sound with an interval analysis composed with a not-equals
analysis; the sound version was faster overall, more accurate on 104 of 289
benchmarks and less accurate on 135. *(The "and less accurate on 135" half was
absent when this entry was written and added 2026-07-22 — the source's very next
sentence, omitted, which made an even accuracy trade read as a win.)*
Its equality stays exact over ℝ with the interval facts riding alongside — the same
separation, arrived at independently.

**Outcome.** `/physics` is confirmed **scorer-only**: γ̂ never evolves in the
oracle, which resolved a standing contradiction (`library-landscape` and the README
already denied trajectories while `gamma-hat §2` listed time-stepping as a γ̂ write
path). Drift is *exported*, not dissolved — the steppable-form manifest now carries
the encoding validity domain, and names the BUG family whose guarantees match its
existing `conserve | bound | monotone` obligation map. The open register lost the
four γ̂ questions and three of four verifier-soundness gaps; the survivors are
stated sharply rather than deleted. Traps 63–65.

## 2026-07-21 — Hardening the book against its own restructure

The restructure above was six bulk transformations — 56 pages moved, 11 process
artifacts collapsed, 25 formulas renamed, 19 rows retagged, ~97 graph edges derived,
~40 path citations rewritten — and none of them had been verified. Six adversarial
skeptics were pointed at the transformations rather than at the physics, one per
transformation, briefed to find damage rather than confirm success.

**Outcome.** About forty defects, all in the seam between what a transformation
intended and what it did.

- **Ten registry rows** wrongly tagged or named. Two of the retags would have shipped
  a *certificate* for a gradient that does not exist: rows 46 and 50 are piecewise
  constant, so the vJp-vs-JvP gate passes on their zero gradient. Three more —
  rows 5, 13, 36 — are converged fixed points that were tagged `D2`, which exposed a
  defect in the vocabulary itself: `D3` was written as an alternative to `D2` and so
  was *weaker* than it, letting a fixed-point row avoid the gate by being tagged more
  precisely. `D3` is now a refinement of `D2` — same gate, plus a conditioning check
  (`τ_cond`) on the fixed-point Jacobian, which the adjoint gate structurally cannot
  see because both of its sides solve against the same bad Jacobian.
- **The tag `DX` renamed `DN`** — it collided with the DX center, a deep-donor
  configuration this corpus analyses in AlGaN. `GAP` was found to mean three things.
- **Both checkers were part of the defect.** `apparatus.py` validated only bracketed
  ids matching `arch|impl|mvp|deriv`, leaving most of the corpus's ids unchecked; its
  dated-anchor pattern skipped every `[timeline] §<date>` citation; and it never
  validated the parenthetical that disambiguates the three entries sharing 2026-06-10.
  `seams.py` captured `formula =` arguments with an ASCII-only class. The
  `depends-on` edge criterion was documented as derivable and derived by nobody;
  deriving it found 33 missing edges (325 → 358). `journal/instructions.md` — the file an agent
  reads first — was checked by nothing and was three restructures stale.
- **The citation layer** had been rewritten mechanically into targets that did not
  carry what the citations reached for: every `[Wave-1 A#]` tag and every
  `gap-audit <letter><number>` citation was orphaned. Both index tables are restored
  here (above), which is where they belong now that the artifacts are gone.
- **Three seeded values** survived only in git history and are re-seeded into the
  reference CSVs; three verifier-soundness gaps are now named in
  [open-decisions] rather than left absent, because an absent check reads
  exactly like a passing one.
- **The implementation-language decision was reopened.** Canon recorded it closed and
  prescribed four languages; it is not closed. The decision is split at its real seam:
  the four-role polyglot *shape* stays closed, the *picks* are open, and every
  language mention is now a candidate carrying the requirement it was chosen to
  satisfy.

**Calibrated certification.** Six auditors were then run over disjoint invariant
classes — counts, signs/units/frames, citations, completeness claims, topic
ownership, and registry tags — each first calibrated against a defect planted in a
scratch copy that both checkers passed clean. **Four of six found their exact
plant; two found a *different real defect* in the same class and reported it as
the plant.** That is a 4/6 gate, not 6/6, and it is recorded as such: their eye
for the class is demonstrably real, but their live results are not exhaustive.
Both of the "wrong" finds were genuine — a glossary claim that a lint check
existed when none did, and a trap marked `enforced` at a page that did not state
its rule.

They raised 54 findings. 66 skeptics tried to refute each one; **35 survived, 19
were killed.** The survivors included a hole current written for the electron
field, an inverted Cowley-Sze pinning parameter, a stress sign against both the
stress theorem and the page's own flow equation, two registry rows that could not
form their declared outputs from their declared inputs, and — repeatedly — values
restated away from their owners and drifted, including two that standing traps
exist specifically to forbid. About a third were residue from this pass's own
earlier edits, unswept.

**Method note.** Every checker was calibrated against a planted defect of its own
class before any clean run was believed — 15 probes, 15 fired, and two of the new
checks missed on the first attempt and were fixed before the result was accepted.
That discipline is now trap 58, and it caught a wrong count in this pass's own
commit message.

## 2026-07-22 — Re-audit: the calibration itself

Asked to re-explore the base from scratch and treat every prior claim as
unverified. Two findings were structural, and both were about *evidence* rather
than content.

**The calibration did not do what canon said it did.** `10.1-conventions` and
`instructions.md` both stated that `calibrate.py` "plants one defect per check",
and "29/29 fired" was cited corpus-wide as the warrant for believing a green run.
Four of `seams.py`'s fourteen finding classes had no probe at all —
`formula-name`, `tolerance`, `glossary`, `refdata-source` — and nine
`apparatus.py` checks likewise, including duplicate `id`, a missing required
field, and a page filed under the wrong chapter. Every one of those thirteen
checks *worked*: all thirteen new probes fired on the first attempt. The defect
was never in the checkers, it was in what the calibration established. Trap 58,
one level up, is now trap 66.

The repair is not the thirteen probes. Coverage is now **derived** from
`seams.py`'s source and asserted at the end of every run, so a check nothing
reaches fails the calibration instead of passing silently. Verified by planting an
unreachable check and confirming the gate reports it.

**A distribution that adds up has not been checked.** `[computational-overview]`
§6.3 gave the cost-tier split as 75/40/13/4 against an actual 76/40/11/5 — wrong
in three of four entries, and summing to 132 either way, so every check of the
total passed. The diff tally on the next line was correct because a checker
compared it to the CSV. Now the tier line has one too. Trap 67.

**A row of reference data had silently shifted its columns.** An unquoted comma in
a `Source` cell split `transport-coefficients.csv` line 66 into twelve fields
against a ten-column header; `Added` held `experimental` and `Modified` held `1`.
Both checkers passed it, because the cells they read *by name* were still
non-empty — they just held the wrong values. That file outranks canon for seeded
coefficients. Arity and date sanity are now checked.

**Residue, swept.** `[computational-overview]` contradicted itself: §3.2 said the
four γ̂ questions were resolved while §13 — the section billed as the catalog of
what is *intentionally open* — still listed them as genuinely open and
cross-referenced the §3.2 that says the opposite. The research brief had inherited
it, so R2.4 announced a "GENUINELY OPEN PROBLEM" that had closed and R7.3 quoted
canon as saying something canon disclaimed in the same paragraph. Five
present-tense references to `lint.py`, deleted months ago, including a glossary
row crediting it with a check that now lives in `apparatus.py` — the second
instance of that exact sentence to be caught. A botched global substitution
leaving "the the canon chapters tree". And, from the previous day's own commit,
"Three concurrent decisions" above a list of four.

Six new checks, all calibrated: cost-tier distribution, reference-data arity,
reference-data dates, the probe count itself, the vocabulary-count constants
anchored to their owner pages' titles, and the coverage gate. 48 probes.

**Method note.** The probe count drifted from 45 to 47 to 48 *during this pass*,
one commit after landing a trap about exactly that. It was caught each time by the
check added for it, which is the only reason this note can state the number.
